# TaskFlow — User Personas

---

## 1. Priya Sharma — The Student Contributor

**Age:** 20 | **Role:** Computer Science undergraduate | **Tech comfort:** High

**Context:** Priya is one of four students on a semester-long capstone project. Her group currently coordinates over WhatsApp and a shared Google Doc, which makes it hard to tell who's doing what.

**Goals**
- See exactly what she's responsible for and by when
- Avoid duplicate work with teammates
- Get credit for her contributions when grades are partly based on participation

**Frustrations**
- Tasks get "assigned" in chat and forgotten
- No single source of truth for deadlines
- Hard to tell if a teammate is stuck or just hasn't started

**Needs from TaskFlow**
- A personal "My Tasks" view across the project
- Clear due dates and priority
- Lightweight — no steep learning curve before the next deadline

---

## 2. Marcus Webb — The Club/Team Lead (Admin)

**Age:** 21 | **Role:** President of a university robotics club | **Tech comfort:** Medium

**Context:** Marcus manages 15 club members across 3 concurrent sub-projects (competition robot, outreach event, sponsorship deck). He needs visibility without micromanaging.

**Goals**
- See overall progress across all sub-projects at a glance
- Make sure nothing slips before the competition deadline
- Onboard new members quickly each semester

**Frustrations**
- Spreadsheets go stale within a week
- No way to see who's overloaded vs. idle
- Re-explaining "the system" to every new member

**Needs from TaskFlow**
- Admin-level dashboard/analytics across projects
- Easy invite flow for new members
- Role controls so only officers can restructure boards

---

## 3. Alicia Chen — The Freelancer

**Age:** 29 | **Role:** Independent UX designer, 4 concurrent clients | **Tech comfort:** High

**Context:** Alicia juggles multiple client projects simultaneously and needs to keep them cleanly separated while still working efficiently across all of them.

**Goals**
- Switch between client projects without mental overhead
- Track deadlines across all clients in one place
- Look professional if a client is invited in as a Viewer

**Frustrations**
- Losing track of which deliverable is due for which client
- Tools that assume one big company, not many small isolated projects
- No good way to share progress with a client without giving them edit access

**Needs from TaskFlow**
- Multiple workspaces/projects with clean separation
- A Viewer role for clients
- Fast project switching, due-date visibility across projects

---

## 4. Jordan Lee — The Startup/Dev Team Engineer

**Age:** 26 | **Role:** Backend engineer, 5-person startup | **Tech comfort:** Very high

**Context:** Jordan's team ships weekly. They need a Kanban flow that mirrors their dev process (To Do → In Progress → In Review → Done) with fast, low-friction updates.

**Goals**
- Move tasks through states quickly during standups
- Get notified when mentioned in a comment or assigned a bug
- Trust that the board reflects reality in real time, since teammates update it live

**Frustrations**
- Stale boards that don't reflect what's actually happening
- Context switching between chat and task tool for the same conversation
- Manual refreshing to see if a task moved

**Needs from TaskFlow**
- Real-time sync — no manual refresh
- Comments with @mentions tied directly to tasks
- Fast keyboard-friendly interactions
